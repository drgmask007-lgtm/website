Internal Combustion (IC) engines power most vehicles on the road today.
They convert chemical energy from fuel into mechanical work through controlled explosions.
Here is a quick look at how they function and why they remain relevant.



The Four-Stroke Cycle

How They Work: The Four-Stroke Cycle

Most modern cars use a four-stroke process to generate power:

  1. Intake: The engine draws in a mixture of air and fuel.
  2. Compression: A piston tightly compresses this mixture to increase energy potential.
  3. Power: A spark plug ignites the fuel, causing an explosion that forces the piston down
  4. Exhaust: The engine vents the spent burned gases out of the tailpipe.

Key Advantages

  • High Energy Density: Fossil fuels pack massive energy into a small volume.
  • Quick Refueling: Recharging a liquid fuel tank takes only a few minutes.
  • Established Infrastructure: Gas stations exist globally, making long travel easy.

Main Types of IC Engines

Engines are generally classified by how they ignite their fuel.

  • Spark Ignition (SI):These run on petrol and use spark plugs.
  • Compression Ignition (CI): These run on diesel and use high pressure to ignite fuel.
